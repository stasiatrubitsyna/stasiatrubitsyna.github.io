Definitions of robots

'Robot' comes from the Czech word Robota meaning serf or slave – it was defined in Karel Capek’s drama Rossum’s Universal Robots, RUR, 1921

Robot Institute of America definition: “A robot is a re-programmable multi-functional manipulator designed to move materials, parts, tools or specialised devices, through variable programmed motions for the purpose of a variety of tasks.” However there are some more controversial definitions like the Datatronics.net definition: “A program that automatically searches the world wide web for files” Or Douglas Adams’, in Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy: “Your plastic pal who is fun to be with!” There are lots of different ways of thinking of robots.
